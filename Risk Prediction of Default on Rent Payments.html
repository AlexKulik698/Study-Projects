Under first Confusion Matrix I have limited the maximum amount of leafs and depth of our Decision Tree Classifier. Having a limited amount of depth allowed us to increase our accuracy as limited amount of depth prevents our model to be overfitted. In my second test I have decided to allow our Decision Tree to grow tall with a max depth of 25, and no limit to the number of leafs. This in turn have overfitted our model and substantially decreased the accuracy of our model by roughly 8%.

How does changing hyperparms effect model performance?

Hyperparms allow us to adjust the model to have the most effective accuracy, without overfitting the model. Grid Search and Param Search are instrumental in us achievinig the most reliable combination of parameters without guesswok involved.

Why do you think certain models performed better/worse?

Some models are fit better or worse for different types of data sets. For example some data with binary attributes might be less fit for a classifier that excels in predicting floating-point number.

How does this performance line up with known strengths/weakness of these models?

All models ended up having very similar accuracy however Random Forest Classifier graph have given us insight of how each model changes with the amount of data presented to it. While Extra Trees Classifier spikes in accuracy early on, Random Forest classifier shows us a steady growth in accuracy that does not drop off nearly as fast as the ladder.
